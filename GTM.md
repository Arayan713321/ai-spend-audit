# GTM.md (Go-To-Market Strategy)

## Target User
**The "Fragmented Founder"**: A technical founder or early-stage CTO at a 5–20 person startup. 
- They have high AI tool usage (Cursor, Claude, GPT-4o).
- They are busy and haven't audited their SaaS spend in 6+ months.
- They feel a vague "subscription fatigue" but don't have the data to justify a switch.

## User Behavior Context
They are likely scrolling through **X (Twitter)** or **Hacker News** looking for productivity hacks or "state of the market" AI news. They just paid their monthly credit card bill and saw a $4,000 "AI/Software" line item that made them wince.

## Distribution Channels
1. **The "X" Viral Loop**: Post the tool on X with a screenshot of a "Big Savings" result. Tag AI influencers (e.g., Greg Kamradt, Rowan Cheung). The "Shareable URL" with OG tags is designed for this.
2. **Niche Communities**:
   - **Indie Hackers / BuildSpace**: Where founders are price-sensitive.
   - **Product Hunt**: Launching as a "Free Tool for the Community."
   - **Reddit (r/startups, r/programming)**: Focus on the "Audit Logic" transparency (citing official pricing).
3. **The First 100 Users ($0 budget)**:
   - Cold-DM 20 founders on X who have "AI" in their bio.
   - Post a "Show HN" on Hacker News with a focus on the deterministic audit logic.
   - Share in the "Cursor" and "Anthropic" Discord/Forum communities as a "Plan Optimization Guide."

## Unfair Distribution Channel
**Credex's Internal Data**: Leveraging Credex's existing knowledge of where credits apply best allows this tool to provide "insider" recommendations that generic calculators can't.

## Week-1 Traction Targets
- 500 Unique Visitors.
- 150 Completed Audits.
- 15 Shared Results.
- 5 Qualified Leads for Credex.
